# Tectonic Plate Boundary Research

## 2-Plate Scenarios (Classic Boundaries)

### 1. San Andreas Fault (Transform)
- **Plates**: Pacific Plate + North American Plate
- **Type**: Transform (conservative) boundary
- **Location**: California, USA
- **Motion**: Right-lateral strike-slip, ~46mm/year
- **Notable**: 1300km long, extends 25km deep

### 2. Mid-Atlantic Ridge (Divergent)
- **Plates**: North American Plate + Eurasian Plate (north), South American Plate + African Plate (south)
- **Type**: Divergent boundary
- **Location**: Atlantic Ocean floor
- **Motion**: Spreading ~2.5cm/year
- **Notable**: Longest mountain range on Earth

### 3. Himalayan Collision Zone (Convergent)
- **Plates**: Indian Plate + Eurasian Plate
- **Type**: Convergent (continental-continental)
- **Location**: Nepal, Tibet
- **Motion**: India moving north ~5cm/year
- **Notable**: Created highest mountains on Earth

## 3-Plate Scenarios (Triple Junctions)

### 1. Afar Triple Junction (RRR - Ridge-Ridge-Ridge)
- **Plates**: Nubian Plate + Somali Plate + Arabian Plate
- **Type**: Three divergent boundaries meeting
- **Location**: Ethiopia/Djibouti (Afar Depression)
- **Motion**: All three plates moving apart
- **Notable**: Clearest triple junction on Earth, active rifting visible

### 2. Mendocino Triple Junction (TTF - Trench-Trench-Fault)
- **Plates**: Pacific Plate + North American Plate + Gorda Plate
- **Type**: Transform + Subduction boundaries
- **Location**: Northern California coast
- **Motion**: Complex interaction, Gorda subducting
- **Notable**: Recent research shows 5 moving pieces

### 3. Chile Triple Junction (RTT - Ridge-Trench-Trench)
- **Plates**: Nazca Plate + Antarctic Plate + South American Plate
- **Type**: Ridge subducting under continent
- **Location**: Southern Chile coast
- **Motion**: Chile Ridge being subducted
- **Notable**: Active ridge subduction

## 4-Plate Scenarios

**Key Finding**: True stable quadruple junctions are theoretically unstable and convert back to triple junctions. However, there are regions where 4 plates interact in close proximity:

### 1. Afro-Arabia Quadruple Junction (Evolving)
- **Plates**: Nubian + Somali + Arabian + possibly Danakil microplate
- **Type**: Evolving from triple to quadruple
- **Location**: Afar region
- **Status**: Transitional, not stable

### 2. Mendocino Complex (Pseudo-Quadruple)
- **Plates**: Pacific + North American + Gorda + Juan de Fuca fragments
- **Type**: Multiple microplates interacting
- **Location**: Northern California/Oregon
- **Status**: Complex multi-plate interaction

## Visualization Approach

For the simulation:
1. **2-Plate**: San Andreas (transform), Mid-Atlantic (divergent), Himalayas (convergent)
2. **3-Plate**: Afar Triple Junction (RRR type) - clearest example
3. **4-Plate**: Show as "complex zone" with Mendocino or theoretical quadruple
